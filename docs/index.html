A Sinking and Exposed City: Climate Stress towards Smarter HCMC

Ho Chi Minh City's climate risks rarely arrive one at a time. Flooding, land subsidence, heat stress, and air quality often build on each other—shaped by low-lying terrain, rapid urban growth, and uneven access to protective infrastructure. This story map shows where these pressures concentrate, and what that means on the ground.

Resources

Population and urban density from WorldPop, buildings and infrastructure from OpenStreetMap, land cover from ESA WorldCover, surface temperature from Landsat 8-9, air pollution from Sentinel-5P and NASA, subsidence from Sentinel-1 InSAR, precipitation from CHIRPS, and flood data from JRC Global Surface Water.

Method

Subsidence tracked via InSAR and ground-based NSS benchmarks. Flood monitoring integrates real-time water levels with drainage networks. Air quality mapped through open monitoring networks with community participation. Urban heat analyzed using long-term Landsat LST series, paired with passive design codes for mitigation.
